Research Interests

My passion lies in modeling biological systems to better understand life and protect it. Here you will find some of the topics I am deeply interested in, although defining the precise scope of my research interests can be challenging at this stage.

Core Research Themes & Methodologies

Population Ecology

  • Population Viability Analysis (PVA)
    I am interested in using PVA to model extinction risks, as it is a powerful tool for translating ecological data into actionable conservation strategies for threatened species.
  • Metapopulation and Spatial Dynamics
    Exploring how species persist in fragmented landscapes through local extinction and colonization events is fascinating because it reveals the critical role of habitat connectivity in long-term survival.
  • Eco-evolutionary Dynamics
    I find it compelling to study how ecological interactions (like competition) and evolutionary changes (like adaptation) feedback on each other in real-time, challenging the traditional view of these as separate processes.
  • Connectivity and Dispersal Processes
    Investigating how individuals move between populations is key to understanding gene flow, resilience, and the potential for recolonization, which are fundamental for both natural systems and conservation planning.

Community Ecology

  • Eco-evolutionary Dynamics
    Applying an eco-evolutionary lens to communities is interesting because it helps explain rapid shifts in biodiversity and ecosystem function, such as how predator-prey arms races can reshape entire food webs.
  • Trophic Webs
    Mapping and modeling trophic webs intrigues me as it uncovers the complex network of energy flow and indirect interactions that determine an ecosystem's stability and response to disturbance.
  • Connectivity and Dispersal Processes
    At the community level, studying dispersal is fascinating because it governs species assembly, influences competitive hierarchies, and can lead to surprising cascading effects throughout the ecological network.

Biological Invasions

  • Invasion Dynamics
    I want to study the non-linear stages of invasion—introduction, establishment, and spread—as understanding the drivers at each phase offers the best chance to develop effective biosecurity and control measures.
  • Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)
    I am drawn to using SEM for invasion biology because it allows for the powerful statistical testing of complex, hypothetical causal pathways linking environmental drivers, invader traits, and ecosystem impacts.
  • Species Distribution Modeling
    Applying SDMs to invasive species is particularly interesting for forecasting their potential spread under different climatic and land-use scenarios, which is crucial for proactive management.

Biogeography

  • Island Biogeography
    The theory of island biogeography provides an elegant, testable framework for understanding fundamental processes of colonization, speciation, and extinction, which is applicable not just to real islands but to any isolated habitat.

Applied Focus

Marine ecology

I aim to direct my future research toward marine ecosystems, a field offering stimulating scientific challenges and growing ecological urgency. The physical complexity of the oceans, their three-dimensional dynamics, and the relative lack of knowledge about many processes particularly attract me.

Key themes : Larval connectivity, trophic cascades, eco-evolutionary dynamics in the context of large-scale dispersal, and spatial modeling integrating climate pressures and the effectiveness of marine protected areas.

Anthropogenic Pressures

I also wish to deepen my modeling of the impacts of human activities on ecosystems. Understanding these interactions, whether habitat fragmentation, overexploitation, or various forms of pollution is, in my view, fundamental to envisioning sustainable management.

Ultimate goal : Contribute to developing predictive tools for evaluating mitigation and adaptation scenarios in response to human impacts on natural systems.

Driving Motivation : Informing Conservation & Management

I see this research as a potential contribution to conservation and natural resource management policies. My ambition is to participate in designing measures based on robust data, such as optimizing protected area networks, preventing biological invasions, or adapting to global changes, in order to reconcile biodiversity preservation with human activities.

Ultimately, the goal is to translate ecological understanding into actionable conservation strategies that protect biodiversity while supporting sustainable human development.
